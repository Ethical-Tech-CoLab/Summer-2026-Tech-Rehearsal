02 CULTURAL HERITAGE & PROVENANCE
HOW CAN TECHNOLOGY SUPPORT THE ETHICAL RETURN OF CULTURAL ARTIFACTS?
Giving institutions, source communities, and researchers a shared, verifiable record of an artifact's life across borders.
Contributors
Christine Lumen
Melanie MacKew
Carolina Moron
Yago Rocha
Hannah Zhao
Advisor
Susan de Menil
CONCEPT OVERVIEW
  • A verifiable chain of custody for cultural artifacts — tracing origin, ownership, and looting/repatriation risk from public record to museum floor
  • Pairs public-facing engagement — a stamp-collecting "art passport" for visitors — with backend provenance intelligence that reconstructs an object's ownership history
  • Extends into an autonomous agent that issues a cryptographically signed, tamper-evident Provenance Passport as a portable, verifiable credential
METHODOLOGY
  • Grounds every provenance claim in an authoritative-source allowlist (The Met, Getty, UNESCO, Interpol, Art Loss Register, ICOM) via Tavily search — no claim is produced without a citation
  • Computes a transparent, rules-based confidence score — not AI-generated — that docks points for custody gaps, unverified sources, and valuation anomalies
  • Signs the resulting Provenance Passport as a JSON-LD Verifiable Credential, independently checkable via ecrecover; premium due-diligence lookups are paid for with x402 USDC micropayments
  • Complements the research pipeline with a public-facing "art passport" app — QR-code stamp collection across venues, in English, French, and Italian

Forced Labor Structural Risk Index
Open Dashboard ↗
An interactive map tool showing how national-level risk aggregation hides where vulnerability actually concentrates — combining a 184-country structural-risk score with subnational analysis across ~1,450 regions built from census microdata and disaster-location data, weighting precarity at 70% and shock exposure at 30%, plus remittance-flow and spatial-clustering (LISA) layers.
#StructuralRisk   #ForcedLabor   #SubnationalAnalysis   #MigrationPressure   #SpatialClustering
